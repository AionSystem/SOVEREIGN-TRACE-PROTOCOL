## PERMITTED USES — NO APPROVAL REQUIRED

The following uses are permitted without seeking approval:

**Factual reference:**
You may refer to this software by its name in factual, descriptive
context. Examples:

- "This project uses the Sovereign Trace Protocol for audit logging."
- "Built on FROZEN-2.0 — the triple-time stamp function."
- "Compatible with the Sovereign Trace Protocol v2.0.0."

**Academic and journalistic use:**
You may use the marks in academic papers, research publications,
news articles, and reviews to identify and discuss this software,
provided the use is accurate and does not imply endorsement.

**Open source attribution:**
Redistributions under Apache 2.0 that preserve attribution notices
per the `NOTICE` file may use the marks to identify the origin.

**Community discussion:**
You may use the marks in technical discussions, forums, and community
spaces to refer to this software accurately.

## PROHIBITED USES

The following uses are prohibited regardless of purpose:

**Misrepresentation of certification status:**
Claiming, implying, or displaying the Sovereign Certified badge
without an active certification license. This is both trademark
infringement and a misrepresentation to clients who rely on
certification status in procurement decisions.

**Competitive confusion:**
Using "Sovereign Trace Protocol," "Sovereign Certified," or
confusingly similar names for a competing product or service
designed to exploit the recognition of these marks.

**False endorsement:**
Using these marks in a way that implies endorsement, partnership,
or affiliation with Sheldon K. Salmon or the Sovereign Trace
Protocol without authorization.

**Trademark registration by third parties:**
Filing trademark applications for these marks or confusingly
similar marks in any jurisdiction. Such filings are made with
constructive notice of this prior use record and will be opposed.

**Disparaging use:**
Using these marks in a manner that is defamatory, obscene,
or designed to harm the reputation of the Sovereign Trace
Protocol or its author.

## CORRECT USAGE GUIDELINES

When using the marks in permitted contexts:

**Capitalization:** Use the marks as shown above — all caps for
SOVEREIGN TRACE PROTOCOL and SOVEREIGN CERTIFIED; standard
capitalization for "Sovereign Trace Protocol" in prose.

**No modification:** Do not shorten, combine, or modify the marks
in ways that could create confusion. "STP" as an abbreviation
in technical context is acceptable; "SovTrace" or other invented
variants are not.

**Attribution:** In publications, include an attribution notice:
"Sovereign Trace Protocol is a trademark of Sheldon K. Salmon."

**First use in a document:** On first use of a mark in a document,
use the ™ symbol: Sovereign Trace Protocol™.

## ENFORCEMENT

Sheldon K. Salmon monitors use of these marks and will enforce
trademark rights against unauthorized use through:

- Cease and desist correspondence
- Domain name dispute proceedings (UDRP / ACPA)
- Opposition proceedings at the USPTO upon registration
- Legal action for infringement under 15 U.S.C. § 1125(a)

Enforcement does not require registration. Common law rights are
actionable immediately upon evidence of confusing use in commerce.
